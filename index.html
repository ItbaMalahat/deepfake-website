Our Mission

Building culturally grounded deepfake research for South Asia

The South Asia Deepfake Detection Challenge is a multi-country research initiative to build culturally grounded datasets, benchmarks, and tools for AI-generated media. Our goal is to advance deepfake detection that reflects the region’s languages, aesthetics, and socio-political realities.

Participating Countries
🇵🇰 Pakistan 🇮🇳 India 🇧🇩 Bangladesh 🇳🇵 Nepal 🇱🇰 Sri Lanka

Research Objectives

What this initiative is building

Representative Multimodal Dataset

Collect image, video, audio, and text-based deepfakes that reflect South Asian languages, cultural cues, political contexts, and real-world manipulation styles.

Regional Benchmarks

Design benchmark tasks and evaluation protocols that capture local challenges such as low-quality media, mobile-edited fakes, code-mixing, and culturally specific manipulations.

Model Development and Comparison

Train and compare deepfake detection models optimized for regional faces, languages, and contexts, and evaluate them against existing global tools.

User-Facing Tools

Build accessible interfaces for journalists and the public, including browser tools, mobile prototypes, and practical fact-checking utilities.
